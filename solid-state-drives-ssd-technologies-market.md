<p><p>Solid-state drives (SSDs) come in various market types, primarily classified as SLC (Single-Level Cell), MLC (Multi-Level Cell), and TLC (Triple-Level Cell). SLC stores one bit per cell, offering the highest speed and endurance, making it ideal for enterprise applications. MLC stores two bits per cell, balancing cost and performance for consumer use. TLC, which stores three bits per cell, provides higher storage density at a lower cost but has slower speeds and lower endurance, suitable for general storage needs.</p></p>
