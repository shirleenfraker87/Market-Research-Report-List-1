<p><p>Polypropylene (PP) is a versatile thermoplastic polymer used in various applications. It exists in three main types based on its crystalline structure: isotactic polypropylene (iPP) has a regular arrangement of methyl groups, offering high strength and thermal resistance; syndiotactic polypropylene (sPP) features alternating methyl group positioning, providing flexibility and impact resistance; while atactic polypropylene (aPP) is amorphous, resulting in lower crystallinity and making it rubber-like. These variations cater to diverse market needs across packaging, automotive, and textile industries.</p></p>
