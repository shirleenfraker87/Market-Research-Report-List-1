<p><p>The influenza vaccine market comprises primarily two types: trivalent and quadrivalent vaccines. Trivalent influenza vaccines protect against three virus strains, typically two influenza A strains and one B strain. In contrast, quadrivalent influenza vaccines offer broader protection against four strains, incorporating an additional B strain. This expansion aims to enhance efficacy and coverage, addressing the diverse and evolving nature of influenza viruses. Both vaccine types play a critical role in public health efforts to reduce influenza-related morbidity and mortality.</p></p>
