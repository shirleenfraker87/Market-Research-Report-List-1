<p><p>The two-phase immersion cooling market is divided into water-based and oil-based systems. Water-based cooling utilizes water as the working fluid, capitalizing on its high thermal conductivity and efficiency for heat transfer. This method is often preferred for data centers and high-performance computing due to its effectiveness and lower cost. In contrast, oil-based cooling uses specially formulated liquids, providing excellent dielectric properties and enabling effective cooling in environments with extreme heat. Each method offers unique advantages tailored to specific cooling needs.</p></p>
