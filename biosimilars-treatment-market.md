Recombinant glycosylated proteins, on the other hand, mimic complex biological functions by including carbohydrate structures, enhancing efficacy and stability. Recombinant peptides are shorter chains of amino acids that can serve specific therapeutic purposes. Together, these categories offer diverse treatment options in the growing biosimilars market.</p></p>
