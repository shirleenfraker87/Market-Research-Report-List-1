<p><p>The laser brazing market encompasses various welding techniques, primarily fillet and lap welding. Fillet welding involves connecting two parts at an angle, creating a triangular joint, ideal for structural applications due to its strength. In contrast, lap welding joins two overlapping pieces, providing a broad surface area for better heat distribution and strength. These methods are gaining traction in industries demanding high precision and durability, such as automotive and aerospace, driven by the advantages of laser technology, including speed and minimal thermal distortion.</p></p>
