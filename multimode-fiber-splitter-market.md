<p><p>The multimode fiber splitter market comprises various types based on the number of output ports. The 1×2 splitter divides a single input into two outputs, suitable for small networks. The 1×4 splitter extends this to four outputs, while the 1×8 allows for eight, catering to larger applications. The 2×2 splitter, using two inputs to connect to two outputs, is ideal for redundancy or load balancing. Other types include configurations like 1×16 or customized splitters for specific needs, enhancing network flexibility.</p></p>
