<p><p>The dried soup market includes two main types: dehydrated dried soup and instant dried soup. Dehydrated dried soup is produced by removing moisture from soup ingredients, requiring rehydration before consumption. This type retains flavor and nutrients but takes longer to prepare. Instant dried soup, on the other hand, is designed for quick preparation and typically requires just the addition of hot water. It often features pre-cooked ingredients, providing convenience for consumers seeking quick meal solutions.</p></p>
