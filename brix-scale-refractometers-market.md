<p><p>The Brix scale refractometer market is divided into traditional and digital types. Traditional refractometers use manual measurement techniques, requiring users to visually interpret readings from a glass prism, typically offering simplicity and durability. In contrast, digital refractometers provide automated readings through electronic sensors, ensuring accuracy and ease of use. Digital models often feature additional functionalities, such as temperature compensation and data logging, making them more suitable for professional applications, while traditional models remain popular for their low cost and straightforward design.</p></p>
