<p><ul><li>Liquefaction Terminal</li><li>Regasification Terminal</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) infrastructure market includes two main types: liquefaction terminals and regasification terminals. Liquefaction terminals are facilities that convert natural gas or light hydrocarbons into liquid form, enabling easier storage and transportation. They are typically located near production sites. In contrast, regasification terminals process the stored liquefied gas back into its gaseous form for distribution and use. These terminals are essential for connecting supply sources to end-users, facilitating efficient energy delivery and supporting various industrial applications.</p></p>
