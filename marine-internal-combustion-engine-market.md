<p><p>The marine internal combustion engine market is categorized into three main types based on speed: low-speed, medium-speed, and high-speed engines. Low-speed engines typically operate at less than 300 RPM and are ideal for large vessels, offering high fuel efficiency and reliability. Medium-speed engines operate between 300 to 1,000 RPM, providing a balance of power and efficiency for various ship sizes. High-speed engines exceed 1,000 RPM and are commonly used in smaller vessels, characterized by quick response and reduced weight.</p></p>
