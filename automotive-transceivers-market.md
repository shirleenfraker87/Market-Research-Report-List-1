<p><p>The automotive transceiver market includes various communication protocols: LIN (Local Interconnect Network) is cost-effective for low-speed, simple applications like sensors. CAN (Controller Area Network) is widely used for reliable, medium-speed communication in powertrain and body control systems. FlexRay supports high-speed, deterministic data transfer, ideal for advanced driver-assistance systems (ADAS). Ethernet enables high-bandwidth, scalable communication for infotainment and autonomous driving. Each protocol caters to specific automotive needs, balancing speed, complexity, and cost, ensuring seamless integration of electronic systems in modern vehicles.</p></p>
