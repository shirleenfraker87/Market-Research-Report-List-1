<p><p>A Grid-to-Vehicle (V2G) system is a technology that enables bidirectional energy flow between electric vehicles (EVs) and the power grid. This capability supports grid stability, reduces peak load demands, and promotes the integration of renewable energy sources by acting as a distributed energy storage system.</p><p>The V2G system market is experiencing significant growth, driven by the increasing adoption of electric vehicles, advancements in smart grid technologies, and the growing need for sustainable energy solutions. These trends highlight the potential of V2G systems to revolutionize energy management and support the transition to a cleaner, more resilient energy ecosystem.</p></p>

<p><p>The **Grid-to-Vehicle (V2G) System** market types include **Centralized**, **Autonomous**, and **Micronet-based** models. In a **Centralized** market, a single entity (e.g., utility or grid operator) manages energy flow between vehicles and the grid, optimizing for grid stability. **Autonomous** markets allow individual vehicles or owners to independently trade energy, leveraging smart contracts or decentralized platforms. **Micronet-based** markets operate within localized grids (e.g., campuses or communities), enabling peer-to-peer energy exchange while maintaining grid independence. Each model balances control, flexibility, and scalability in V2G integration.</p></p>
